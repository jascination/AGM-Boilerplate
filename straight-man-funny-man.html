Straight Man / Funny Man

Today's course is one of our favourites for teaching our clients - a unique system we developed to help our clients understand how to put together outfits that look unique but don't go too over-the-top.

??? In our first lesson we talked about trying to look at your clothes as an entity - as a wardrobe - rather than individual pieces. ????. The same applies here. When you wear clothes, they don't just sit there on their own. If someone compliments your cool jacket, they're not actually just complimenting the jacket - but the outfit that supports it and allows it to look cool.

A great way to visualise this is similar to a classic comedy duo, where you have a straight man, whose role is to act 'normal' and (not fight for the audience's attention) and a funny man, whose role is to be 'odd', to be wacky, to be the source of interest that makes the act worth watching.

In clothes, as in comedy, if you have only straight-men then your outfits are boring; nothing is different or interesting or attention-grabbing. By the same token, if you only have funny-men, then there's no clean foundation; everything is vying for attention and it becomes overwhelming.

The 'funny man' does not necessarily mean something crazily loud or colourful. While it can certainly be these things, it can also be a piece worn in an interesting way, an unusual texture or a novel combination of two items which work together to form a unique look.
