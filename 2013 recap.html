I spent $200,000 on men's clothes last year

And here's what I learned.

  • James Gallichio in Meta Menswear

For many men, throwing down a few thousand dollars on new clothes in a big deal. Daunting, of course, because of the huge effect that a whole new wardrobe can have on your life, your confidence and people's perception of you.

But what if you're spending a few thousand dollars every day?

For those new to this site, A Good Man's main service is personal styling. We meet clients, assess their needs and budget and help them curate a wardrobe that will help them become the man they feel like on the inside. Sounds cheesy, I know, but it's a huge transformation and a pretty sweet job.

That's a lot of clients, a lot of money and a lot of guys feeling great about themselves. That $200,000 is money we don't earn commission on; all cash that goes directly to small retail businesses.

But enough stats. Here's a list of things we learned after trying on pretty much every item of clothing in Australia.

  • Fat Men Don' thave Fat Legs
  • Evertibe us 4 sizes smaller than they think
  • Colour theory is mostly bullshit
  • Men have trouble letting go of clothes (sentimental vs stuff they actually wear)
  • Everyone looks good in Filippa K Chinos
  • Men want to be comfortable: the 2014 look
  • Men want minimal effort in their wardrobe
  • We all have a fashion nemesis: the well-dressed guy that we hate
  • Shopping at bricks-and-mortar stores is way less stressful than men think it will be: "In defence of shopping offline"
  • Pants set the occasion
  • Changing a wardrobe is a therepeutic experience. A lot of stuff comes out in the process.
  • You have to give a shit before you can not give a shit: on knowing the rules before you break them
  • Everyone has a "thing" they're self-conscious about re.clothes
  • Intimidation in high-end stores -> do a photoshoot in Harrolds
  • Clothing is about relationships. Tailor, shop owner, etc
