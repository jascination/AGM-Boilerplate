How to be Effective with Colour

One of the most common questions we get from men is: "What colours do I wear?

Colour theory is one of the most complicated aspects of men's style, and for the most part, it's just total bs. Sure, we have the four seasonal colour groups (Spring, Winter, Autumn, Summer). But then there was a need to develop half-season groups (Light Spring, Dark Spring) because people just don't fit into such neat little packages. And even those sub-groups are problematic!

So here's a little trick that we use with our clients - and we use it ourselves - which can be used whenever you want to look like you're using colour effectively in an outfit.

Rule: Wear Three Colours

It's really as simple as that. If you want to look like your outfit uses colours effectively, strip it back to three of them. For pratical purposes a pattern counts as a colour, and if you want to stretch the rule a bit you can make a white shirt or t-shirt not count.
